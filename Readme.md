<h2><a id="overview"></a>Project Overview</h2>

This project presents a comprehensive Hospital Management Dashboard built in Power BI to provide actionable insights into hospital operations, patient management, appointments, treatments, revenue, and doctor performance. The dashboard enables healthcare administrators and stakeholders to monitor key metrics, identify trends, and make data-driven decisions to improve operational efficiency and patient care.

The dashboard is divided into three sections: Executive Overview, Patient & Appointment Analysis, and Doctor, Treatment & Revenue Analysis. Together, these reports help stakeholders make informed decisions and improve hospital efficiency.

<h2><a id="key-features"></a>Key Features</h2>

<h4>1. Executive Overview Dashboard</h4>

Provides a high-level summary of hospital performance through key business metrics and operational insights.

**Highlights:**

* Total Revenue, Patients, Doctors, and Appointments.
* Average Treatment Cost and Average Bill Amount.
* Appointment trends by month.
* Appointment distribution across hospital branches.
* Appointment status analysis (Scheduled,
  Completed, Cancelled, No-show).
* Payment status breakdown.
* Top revenue-generating patients.
* Dynamic filtering by appointment status and hospital branch.

<h4>2. Patient & Appointment Analysis</h4>

Offers detailed insights into patient demographics, registration trends, and appointment behavior.

**Highlights:**

* Patient registration trends by month.
* Insurance provider analysis.
* Gender distribution of patients.
* Appointment attendance rate monitoring.
* Patient segmentation by age group.
* Analysis of visit reasons (Checkup,
  Consultation, Therapy, Emergency, Follow-up). 
* Top patients by appointment count.
* Interactive filters for registration date, gender, insurance 
  provider, and appointment status.

<h4>3. Doctor, Treatment & Revenue Analysis</h4> 

Focuses on doctor performance, treatment utilization, payment preferences, and revenue generation.

**Highlights:**

* Appointments handled by each doctor.
* Revenue generated by doctors.
* Average treatment cost by treatment type.
* Most preferred payment methods.
* Treatment distribution analysis.
* Revenue contribution by medical specialization.
* Interactive filtering by treatment date, doctor, specialization,
  treatment type, and payment method.

<h2><a id="business-problem"></a>Business Problem</h2>

Hospitals generate large volumes of operational data across appointments, patient registrations, treatments, doctors, and billing systems. However, decision-makers often struggle to obtain a consolidated view of performance.

The goal of this project was to create a centralized analytics dashboard that enables hospital management to monitor operations, identify trends, and improve efficiency through data-driven decisions.


<h2><a id="approach"></a>Approach</h2>

I started by understanding the key business requirements and identifying the metrics that hospital administrators would need on a daily basis.

The data was cleaned and transformed using Power Query, followed by creating a structured data model. DAX measures were developed to calculate KPIs such as revenue, appointment counts, attendance rates, treatment costs, and doctor performance metrics.


<h2><a id="business-impact"></a>Business Impact</h2>

This dashboard enables hospital management to:

* Improve appointment scheduling efficiency.
* Optimize doctor utilization.
* Monitor financial performance in real time.
* Identify growth opportunities.
* Enhance patient service quality.

<h2><a id="research-questions"></a>Research Questions</h2>

These questions can be explored using this dashboard and future enhancements.

**Patient Analytics**

1. Which age group visits the hospital most frequently?
2. Which gender has a higher appointment completion rate?
3. What are the most common reasons for hospital visits?
4. Which insurance providers cover the highest number of patients?
5. Which patient segments generate the most revenue?

**Appointment Analytics**

6. What factors contribute to appointment no- shows?
7. Which months experience peak appointment demand?
8. Which branch has the highest appointment volume?
9. How does attendance rate vary across demographics?
10. Which doctors have the highest patient retention?

**Financial Analytics**

11. Which treatments generate the highest revenue?
12. Which specialization is most profitable?
13. What payment methods are preferred by patients?
14. How does treatment cost affect revenue?
15. Which branch contributes the highest revenue?

**Operational Analytics**

16. Which doctors handle the highest patient load?
17. Are doctors evenly distributed across departments?
18. Which services require additional staffing?
19. What operational bottlenecks impact appointment completion?
20. How can hospital resources be optimized?

**Predictive Analytics (Future Scope)**

21. Can patient no-shows be predicted?
22. Which patients are likely to return for
    follow- up visits?
23. What will future appointment demand look like?
24. Which treatments will experience increased demand?
25. Can revenue be forecasted accurately for upcoming quarters?
